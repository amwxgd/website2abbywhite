8 Disney movies that don’t pass the Bechdel Test

The Bechdel Test is a common measure of female interactions in movies. To pass the Bechdel Test a movie is required to contain the following: at least two female characters, that those characters have a conversation with each other, and that their conversation is about something other than a man. There are also additional requirements occasionally added such as that the two female characters are named. While this is not the only determining factor when it comes to good movies, it is certainly something to consider before sitting down to watch.
